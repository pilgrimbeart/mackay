# Sustainable Energy - Without the Hot Air 
“SEWTHA” was David’s attempt to bring the energy conversation back to first principles: quantify demand, quantify supply, and see what combinations of technologies can plausibly add up.
Instead of arguing from slogans, it invites the reader to do the arithmetic: using plain units, transparent assumptions, and comparisons on a per-person basis, to test which options are big enough to matter and which are destined to be marginal.
The book’s effect has been unusually direct for a work of science: it equipped non-specialists to participate intelligently in policy debates, gave engineers and campaigners a shared numerical framework, and helped shift public discussion toward the “numbers, not adjectives” standard that David championed, raising the quality of energy thinking in classrooms, boardrooms, and government alike.

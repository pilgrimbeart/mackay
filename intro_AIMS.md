# The African Institute for Mathematical Sciences
AIMS was founded to give talented African graduates an intensive, one-year immersion in the mathematical sciences, taught by visiting lecturers from around the world.

David threw himself into this mission from the start: as the Royal Society memoir records, he became involved at the institute’s launch in South Africa and spent roughly eight weeks per year there across the academic years 2003–06. In the early curriculum he taught “Inference and Information Theory,” bringing his distinctive, confidence-building emphasis on clear reasoning under uncertainty to a cohort of students poised to carry those tools back into their own countries and careers.
